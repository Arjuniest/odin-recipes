Jhal Muri

Description

Jhal muri is an Indian street food/chaat made in Kolkata with puffed rice("Muri"), boiled potato, chopped onions, fresh cucumber, tomatoes, green chillies, coconut, sprouted Bengal gram, etc. All of this is seasoned with a special jhal muri moshla (spice mix), generous sprinkling of pungent mustard oil, and black salt. Jhal muri sellers set up their shops outside schools, colleges, railway stations, parks—anywhere that people need a light, affordable evening snack. Our favourite jhal muris are those served on trains during long journeys, by travelling vendors who board the train with their jhal muri setup, make jhal muri for the hungry (or bored) passengers, and then get off the train at another station. They keep travelling back and forth on certain routes all day selling delicious jhal muri.

Ingredients

  • 30 g muri (puffed rice)
  • 25 g chanachur
  • 15 g plain bhujiya
  • ¼ tsp beetnoon (black salt)
  • 2 g green chillies
  • ¼ tsp ginger
  • 25 g boiled potatoes
  • ¼ Chopped onions
  • 3 g mustard oil
  • 10 g tomatoes
  • 5 g lime juice

Steps:

  1. Before starting, make sure that your muri is very crispy. You can microwave it for 30 seconds if you want.
  2. Boil potatoes. Peel and cut them into 1 cm cubes. Finely chop onions, green chillies, and ginger. Dice the tomatoes, removing the pulpy interior, or the muri will turn soggy. Chop coconut. Make a paste of green chillies.
  3. Take muri in a large mixing bowl. Add chanachur and bhujiya. Add green chillies and ginger. Now, add the boiled and chopped potato. Add coconut. You should add the dry(ish) ingredients first so that the muri does not turn soggy.
  4. Now, add green chilli paste. Drizzle some raw mustard oil. Add onions, tomatoes, and a squeeze lime juice.
  5. Mix quickly and serve.
